# Starter Scenarios

## S001 — One-day math workbook photo
Parent uploads one photo of a 3rd-grade workbook page on equivalent fractions and adds:
> "We just need today. My son gets frustrated if there are too many word problems."

Expected:
- today-only or today + rough next step
- clear parent instructions
- short reinforcing activity
- no fake full-week curriculum expansion

## S002 — Weekly assignment paste
Parent pastes:
> Monday: read chapter 2 and answer 1–4
> Tuesday: vocabulary
> Wednesday: lab video
> Thursday: worksheet
> Friday: quiz review

Expected:
- week plan
- each day mapped clearly
- daily lesson draft for day 1
- follow-on activity only where useful

## S003 — Topic-only elective
Parent types:
> "My 10-year-old is obsessed with bridges. I want a 2-week mini module."

Expected:
- small module skeleton
- strong first day
- project-oriented activity suggestion

## S004 — Mixed-source household
Parent uploads a TOC image and types:
> "We use this book plus YouTube and a local co-op. Fridays are light."

Expected:
- bounded interpretation of the book
- realistic weekly pacing
- recognition of Friday constraint

## S005 — Chess enrichment
Parent types:
> "Teach my 8-year-old pins and skewers. He knows how pieces move but misses tactics."

Expected:
- one clear lesson
- one interactive or puzzle-like activity
- visible fit with chess-style pack behavior

## S006 — Sparse reading intervention
Parent types:
> "2nd grader, behind in reading, 20 minutes today only. Needs confidence."

Expected:
- confidence-building lesson
- short activity
- no over-engineered plan

## S007 — PDF excerpt science
Parent uploads a short PDF excerpt from a life science workbook and types:
> "This is all I have with me right now."

Expected:
- extract key concepts from PDF
- build today cleanly
- avoid inventing full unit structure

## S008 — Two learners, same source
Parent types:
> "Both kids are doing the same history unit, ages 9 and 12. Can you split today's work?"

Expected:
- same source
- differentiated lesson expectations
- avoid collapsing them into one generic lesson

## S009 — Badly formatted input
Parent pastes a messy list copied from notes app with odd bullets, partial dates, and abbreviations.

Expected:
- robust normalization
- clear clarification assumptions
- still produce a bounded result

## S010 — Parent wants more script
Parent types:
> "I do better when you tell me exactly what to say and ask."

Expected:
- more scripted parent instructions
- still concise
- not robotic
