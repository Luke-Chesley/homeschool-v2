# Phase 6: Beta, Metrics, And Go / No-Go

## Purpose

This phase decides whether the wedge is strong enough to launch for real.
The goal is not to collect compliments.
The goal is to measure whether families reach value quickly, come back, and show willingness to pay.

## Outcome

At the end of Phase 6:

- a real beta cohort has used the product
- activation and week-1 retention are measurable
- willingness to pay is no longer hypothetical
- launch risks are documented clearly
- go / no-go is decided from evidence, not vibes

## Beta Shape

Run a small beta with real target households.
Suggested size:

- 15 to 25 households

Prioritize households that match the wedge:

- mixed curriculum inputs
- parent running day-to-day teaching
- at least occasional plan drift
- willing to use the product more than once

## Questions The Beta Must Answer

- Can a fresh household reach Today fast enough?
- Do users trust the generated interpretation of partial inputs?
- Does the product help them recover when a day goes off plan?
- Do they return to Today in the same week?
- Do they add a second learner?
- Do they see tracking as useful or just extra surface area?
- Are they willing to pay after using the real workflow?

## Required Metrics

### Activation

Target metrics to review:

- sign-up to first Today median time
- % of sign-ups that reach first Today
- % of users who open a lesson draft
- % of users who generate or open an activity
- % of users who mark at least one plan item status

Suggested success thresholds:

- at least 60% of signed-up households reach first Today
- median time to first Today under 5 minutes for the fast path
- at least 50% of activated households mark one plan item in the first session

### Week-1 Retention

Target metrics to review:

- day-2 return rate
- day-7 return rate
- Today opens in week 1
- plan item status changes in week 1
- additional curriculum inputs added in week 1
- second learner added in week 1

Suggested success thresholds:

- at least 35% day-7 return for the beta cohort
- at least 3 Today opens in week 1 for a meaningful subset of households
- at least 40% of activated households add more source material or continue planning after the first day

### Monetization

Target metrics to review:

- % who start trial when offered
- % who say they would pay after using the workflow
- % who convert if billing is live
- reasons for non-conversion

Suggested success thresholds:

- at least 30% of beta households indicate credible willingness to pay
- at least 10 paid conversions or strong equivalent founder commitments before scaling broader acquisition

## Qualitative Review Prompts

Ask beta users:

- What did you expect this to do before you tried it?
- What was the first thing that felt useful?
- Where did setup feel heavier than it should?
- Did the generated day feel trustworthy?
- When the plan changed, did the app help or create more work?
- Would you use this next week without being asked?
- What would make this worth paying for?

## Go / No-Go Framework

### Go

Go if most of these are true:

- activation is strong enough that users reach Today quickly
- a meaningful share returns in week 1
- users describe the product as reducing replanning burden
- users trust the partial-input workflow enough to use it again
- some households show willingness to pay without heavy persuasion

### No-Go

No-go if several of these are true:

- users do not reach Today reliably
- the generated plan feels untrustworthy from partial inputs
- parents use it once as a novelty and do not return
- the value feels like generic AI generation, not a durable operating system
- willingness to pay is weak even among the best-fit beta users

### Iterate, Then Re-Test

If the result is mixed:

- cut scope further
- fix the specific activation bottleneck
- re-run with another small cohort

Do not widen the target user just because the initial wedge is hard.

## Suggested Implementation Order

1. wire the instrumentation before recruiting beta users
2. recruit the beta cohort deliberately
3. run onboarding and week-1 observation
4. review quantitative and qualitative data together
5. decide go / no-go explicitly
6. only then consider scaled acquisition or public launch

## Exit Criteria

Phase 6 is complete when:

- the beta cohort has used the product
- the key activation and retention metrics are known
- willingness to pay has been tested
- launch blockers are explicit
- a written go / no-go decision exists
